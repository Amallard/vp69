Ombudsman

In 1970 the Chief of Naval Operations identified a critical deficiency. He understood there was a need for individuals to act as liaison between the command and the command families experiencing difficulties. To meet this need, he established the Navy family Ombudsman Program. Today’s Ombudsmen are the crucial communications link from the command to families, ensuring families’ concerns are heard by the command.

The fighting Totems of Patrol Squadron SIX NINE Command Ombudsman:

Mrs. Evonne Harrison, Mrs. Nichole Rumbaugh, and Mrs. Elizabeth Sies

Patron Six Nine is located at Ault Field on NAS Whidbey Island just outside of Oak Harbor, Washington. If you are reporting to the command or looking for more information about our location, please call the Duty Office to contact the Sponsor Coordinator.

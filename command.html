COMMANDING OFFICER

Commander Jason B. Brookham

Commander Jason B. Brookham, of Falcon, Colorado, earned a Bachelor of Science Degree in Geology from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, Troy, York, and was commissioned an Ensign in 1998. In January 1999 he reported for Naval Flight Officer (NFO) training and was designated a NFO in February 2000. Following initial P-3 training with Patrol Squadron THREE ZERO in Jacksonville, Florida, Commander Brookham reported to the "Fighting Tigers" of Patrol Squadron EIGHT homeported in Brunswick, Maine.

He held several positions at VP-8 including Command Fitness Leader, Aviation Survival Equipment Branch Officer, Readiness Officer and NFO Naval Air Training and Operating Procedures Standardization (NATOPS) Officer. He qualified as a P-3C Tactical Coordinator (TACCO), Mission Commander, Special Projects TACCO and Instructor TACCO. During his tour he completed deployments to Puerto Rico, Iceland and Sigonella, Sicily with multiple detachments throughout the Caribbean and Europe in support of a myriad of operations and exercises including Operation Joint Guardian, Deliberate Forge, Iraqi Freedom and Noble Eagle.

Upon leaving VP-8 in 2004, Commander Brookham reported to Commander Patrol and Reconnaissance Wing FIVE in Brunswick, Maine, in the capacity of Fleet Exercise Officer and Special Projects Department Head. While assigned to CPRW-5, Commander Brookham completed the P-3 Weapons and Tactics Instructor School and became an Advanced Readiness Program instructor. In his off-duty time he earned a Master of Business Administration from Southern New Hampshire University and completed Phase One of the Joint Professional Military Education course through the distance learning program of the U.S. Air Force Air Command and Staff College.

Commander Brookham was released from active duty and joined the "Totems" of Patrol Squadron SIX NINE at Naval Air Station Whidbey Island, Washington, in 2006. He has achieved the qualifications of TACCO, Mission Commander, Instructor TACCO, and TACCO NATOPS evaluator, and served on the ground as the Tactics Officer, Maintenance Officer and Operations Officer. He has completed numerous detachments and the first Reserve Maritime Patrol mobilization to the Seventh Fleet Area of Responsibility in 2013.

In his civilian life, Commander Brookham is a licensed Certified Public Accountant in Arizona, and volunteers with the Civil Air Patrol as a search and rescue pilot. Commander Brookham's personal decorations include the Air Medal (individual award), Navy and Marine Corps Commendation Medal, Navy and Marine Corps Achievement Medal, and various other campaign and unit decorations. Commander Brookham resides in Mesa, Arizona with his wife, Dr. Laura Brookham, a forensic psychologist.

　

EXECUTIVE OFFICER

Commander Peter M. Lauder

CDR Pete Lauder, a native of California, graduated with a Bachelor of Science degree from the United States Naval Academy in 2000 and a Master of Arts degree from Brandman University in 2015. He will become the executive officer for the VP-69 Totems. Operational tours include VP-47 in Kaneohe Bay, HI where he served as an instructor pilot and NATOPS check pilot. For his department head tour with VP-69 he served as both maintenance and operations officer.

Ashore, he served as Fleet NATOPS instructor pilot at the P-3 Fleet Replacement squadron, VP-30 in Jacksonville, FL. Most recently he served as the Reserve VP program manager to Commander Naval Air Force Reserve in San Diego, CA.

Awards include Meritorious Service Medal, Navy Air Medal, and other personal and unit awards. Pete is married to the former Lauren Ihrig of Annandale, VA, a past P-3 instructor pilot. They have two children and live in Anacortes, WA.

　

COMMAND MASTER CHIEF

Master Chief Randall C. Osborn

MCPO(NAC/AW) Osborn entered active duty in the U.S. Navy on 10 April, 1983. After completing recruit training at RTC in San Diego, CA, he reported to Naval Air Technical Training Command in Millington, Tennessee and attended a number of basic and advanced avionics schools. He was promoted to AT3 and accepted orders in June 1984 to the Black Lightning Camp of VP-31 aboard NAS Moffett Field, California. While assigned to VP-31, he completed avionics schools for every version of the P-3C Orion in service at the time, was promoted to AT2, and served as avionics CDQAR and W/C 210 Supervisor. He transferred across the NAS Moffett ramp to The Fighting Marlin’s of VP-40 in 1987, was promoted to AT1, and served as a QAR while completing deployments throughout WESTPAC. AT1 Osborn separated from active service in August of 1989.

Employed by General Dynamics as a F-16A Simulator Field Engineer at Kingsley Field for the Oregon Air National Guard 114th Tactical Fighter Training Squadron, he enlisted in the Naval Reserve and affiliated with the Fighting Totems of VP-69 in September of 1991. He qualified as a P-3C In-flight Technician in 1994, and converted back to active duty as a TAR in June of 1995. He accepted orders to VP-69, and was promoted to Chief Petty Officer in 1996. He served as QA LCPO and Maintenance Control Chief, completing his tour in August of 1999.

Reporting to the Islanders of VR-61 in September of 1999, he remained at NAS Whidbey Island for three more years. He qualified as a C-9B/DC-9 Loadmaster, and served as LCPO for the NATOPS, Operations, and Maintenance departments. He was promoted to ATCS in March of 2002, and transferred to Naval Air Systems Command Headquarters in Patuxent River, Maryland later that same year. While at NAVAIR, he worked on logistical support initiatives for all reserve TYCOM aircraft, as well as development and implementation of NAMP commercial derivative aircraft maintenance policy.

Senior Chief Osborn transferred to the Fighting Saints of VFC-13 aboard NAS Fallon, Nevada in September of 2005 and served as Command Senior Chief of the Navy’s premiere adversary squadron, supporting TOPGUN and multiple fleet and Carrier Air Wing training evolutions for three years. In 2008, he reported aboard VR-58 and served as the Sunseeker Maintenance Senior Chief. He was promoted to AVCM in April of 2010 and accepted orders to CNAFR, San Diego, CA, serving as MMCPO for CNAF Aviation Maintenance Management Team Two. Responsible for standardization and enforcement of naval aviation maintenance policy for every Navy and Marine Corps aviation command world-wide, he inspected aviation maintenance programs ashore and afloat, developed and implemented maintenance policy, and performed the maintenance program Safe For Flight evaluation of the first P-8A Poseidon accepted into the Navy inventory. He was accepted into the Command Master Chief program in 2011, and reported aboard NOSC Kitsap in November 2012 as Command Master Chief responsible for mobilization and readiness for 850 area Selected Reservists. In July 2016, CMDCM Osborn reported again to VP-69 to serve as Command Master Chief for his twilight tour.

MCPO(NAC/AW) Osborn earned his Bachelor of Science in Professional Aeronautics with a minor in management from Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University in 2008, and he attended the Air Force Senior NCO Academy Class 09-B. Master Chief Osborn’s personal awards include the Meritorious Service Medal, Navy and Marine Corps Commendation Medal (five awards), Navy and Marine Corps Achievement Medal (five awards), Navy Good Conduct Medal (seven awards) and various unit, and service awards. MCPO Osborn has also earned one gold and two bronze Excellence-In-Competition badges (Rifleman) during competitions with the US Navy Marksmanship Program. He and his wife Lisa have been married 34 years. Their Son, Randy, lives in Boise, ID, and their Daughter, Jessica, is a Navy Veteran living outside of Kansas City, Kansas.
